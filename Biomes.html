Biomes

A biome is a large geographical area of distinctive plant and animal groups, which are adapted to that particular environment. Biomes are often defined by factors such as climate, vegetation type, and geographical features.

Tropical Rainforest

LRainforests are very wet and warm places where lots of different plants and animals live.

Temperate Rainforest

These are forests where it's not too hot or too cold, and they have lots of trees that change with the seasons.

Grassland

Grasslands are wide open spaces covered in grass instead of trees, where animals like to roam and graze.

Deserts

Deserts are really hot during the day and very cold at night, with lots of sand and not many plants or animals.

Tundra

Tundras are super cold places where the ground is frozen most of the time and there aren't many trees.

Aquatic

Aquatic places are all about water, like oceans, rivers, and lakes, where fish and other water animals live and swim.
